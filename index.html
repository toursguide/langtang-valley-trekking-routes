About the Region

Nepal's Closest Himalayan Wilderness

Langtang Valley lies just 51 kilometres north of Kathmandu yet feels a world apart. Cradled between the main Himalayan chain and the Langtang Lirung massif (7,246m), this glacially sculpted corridor was the third trekking region to be opened in Nepal and remains one of the most accessible alpine landscapes on Earth.

The valley earned the sobriquet "Valley of Glaciers" from explorer Bill Tilman, who first mapped it in 1949. Today it forms the heart of Langtang National Park — Nepal's nearest national park to Kathmandu — sheltering snow leopards, red pandas, Himalayan black bears, and over 250 species of birds beneath the permanent snowfields above.

From a quick 7-day loop to the classic viewpoint at Kyanjin Gompa to a full 14-day circuit crossing sacred Gosaikunda Lake and descending through the Helambu highlands, Langtang offers a remarkable diversity of routes for every trekker. Whether you're planning your first Himalayan adventure or seeking an under-the-radar alternative to more crowded trails, our comprehensive Langtang valley trek packages have everything you need.

The region was devastated by the 2015 Gorkha earthquake and subsequent avalanche, but has been rebuilt with remarkable resilience. Infrastructure is now excellent, teahouse hospitality warm, and trekker numbers still far lower than the Everest or Annapurna regions — making it perfect for those seeking solitude alongside stunning scenery.

The Trails

All Langtang Trekking Routes

From the accessible classic valley walk to challenging high passes and multi-region circuits — choose your adventure below.

01 Most Popular

Classic Langtang Valley Trek

7–9 Days Max 4,773m Moderate

The quintessential Langtang experience. Trekkers follow the Langtang Khola upstream through rhododendron and oak forests, past yak pastures and rebuilt Tamang villages, to the monastery town of Kyanjin Gompa. Stunning views of Langtang Lirung dominate the final days.

  • Syabrubesi → Lama Hotel → Langtang Village → Kyanjin Gompa
  • Hike to Kyanjin Ri (4,773m) for panoramic Himalayan views
  • Visit Kyanjin Monastery and Cheese Factory
  • Return via same route or arrange transport from Syabrubesi
02 Spiritual Route

Langtang–Gosaikunda Trek

10–12 Days Max 4,610m Strenuous

Combines the classic valley walk with a dramatic high-altitude crossing to the sacred Gosaikunda Lake (4,380m), revered by Hindus as the abode of Lord Shiva. The Laurebina Pass (4,610m) offers breathtaking 360° Himalayan views.

  • Extends the classic route over Laurebina Pass (4,610m)
  • Sacred Gosaikunda Lake pilgrimage site
  • Descend via Cholang Pati and Sing Gompa
  • Full immersion in Tamang and Sherpa culture
03 Grand Circuit

Langtang–Helambu Circuit

12–14 Days Max 4,610m Strenuous

The ultimate Langtang adventure. This full circuit links the Langtang Valley with Gosaikunda and the historic Helambu highlands — ending in Sundarijal on the outskirts of Kathmandu for a satisfying point-to-point journey through extraordinary cultural diversity.

  • Three distinct trekking regions in one journey
  • Tamang, Sherpa, and Hyolmo cultural encounters
  • Gosaikunda Lake and Laurebina Pass crossing
  • Finish at Sundarijal, Kathmandu Valley rim
04 Off-the-Radar

Langtang–Tamang Heritage Trail

9–11 Days Max 3,870m Moderate

A culturally immersive alternative that follows the Tamang Heritage Trail before joining the main Langtang route. This circuit showcases the ancient Tamang villages, monasteries, mani walls, and traditional cheesemaking of the lower valleys with fewer crowds.

  • Tamang Heritage Trail loop through villages rarely visited
  • Authentic homestay experiences in Gatlang and Tatopani
  • Hot springs at Tatopani village
  • Connects seamlessly to the main Langtang route

05 Summit Day

Tserko Ri High Camp Extension

8–10 Days Max 4,984m Challenging

An extension of the classic route adding a demanding sunrise climb to Tserko Ri (4,984m). From this magnificent vantage point, you can see Langtang Lirung, Dorje Lakpa, Shishapangma (in Tibet), and on clear days all the way to Everest's plume on the eastern horizon.

  • Pre-dawn summit push for Tserko Ri (4,984m)
  • 360° views spanning Nepal and Tibetan Himalaya
  • Views of 6 peaks above 7,000m from the summit
  • Steep 1,100m ascent — no technical skills required
06 For Adventurers

Langtang–Ganja La Pass Trek

10–12 Days Max 5,122m Very Hard

The most demanding route in the Langtang region. The Ganja La pass crossing (5,122m) requires glacier travel, crampons, and a rope in some seasons. It rewards fit trekkers with a technical adventure connecting the Langtang Valley to the Helambu highlands over a high Himalayan pass.

  • Ganja La Pass at 5,122m — highest crossing in the region
  • Technical section may require crampons and rope
  • Few trekkers; true wilderness experience
  • Guide strongly recommended; solo not advised

Day-by-Day Plan

Classic 9-Day Itinerary

A carefully acclimatized schedule giving you the best of the Langtang Valley with time for side hikes to Kyanjin Ri and Tserko Ri viewpoints.

D1

Kathmandu to Syabrubesi

DRIVE · 117 KM · 7–8 HOURS

▲ 1,460m

Depart Kathmandu early morning by private jeep or local bus via Trishuli Bazaar and Dhunche. The winding mountain road reveals ever-improving Himalayan views as you gain altitude. Arrive Syabrubesi in the afternoon, explore the small market town, and overnight at a teahouse. A short acclimatization walk along the river is recommended.

D2

Syabrubesi to Lama Hotel

TREK · 11 KM · 5–6 HRS

▲ 2,380m · +920m gain

The trail enters Langtang National Park immediately. You climb through dense subtropical forest of bamboo, rhododendron, and giant oaks festooned with orchids. The river gorge narrows dramatically. Wildlife sightings — red pandas, langur monkeys — are common. Overnight at Lama Hotel (also called Ghoda Tabela in some maps).

D3

Lama Hotel to Langtang Village

TREK · 14 KM · 6–7 HRS

▲ 3,430m · +1,050m gain

As the valley opens, the forest gives way to yak pastures and meadows. You pass Ghoda Tabela (military checkpoint) and climb steadily. Langtang Village was completely destroyed in the 2015 earthquake; it has been rebuilt with a touching memorial to the 250+ people lost. The rebuilt stone teahouses are warm and welcoming.

D4

Langtang Village to Kyanjin Gompa

TREK · 6 KM · 2–3 HRS

▲ 3,870m · +440m gain

A short but meaningful day. The trail is gentle, passing through Mundu and Sindum hamlets with incredible mountain framing at every turn. Arrive at Kyanjin Gompa well before noon — time to visit the 700-year-old monastery, sample freshly made yak cheese from the government cheese factory, and take an afternoon acclimatization walk up the ridge. Langtang Lirung (7,246m) towers directly overhead.

D5

Acclimatization: Kyanjin Ri Summit

SIDE HIKE · 4–5 HRS ROUND TRIP

▲ 4,773m · +903m gain

An essential day hike from Kyanjin Gompa. The steep trail to Kyanjin Ri's broad summit rewards with some of the finest views in the entire region — Langtang Lirung, Kimshung, Yansa Tsenji, Naya Kanga, and Gangchenpo fill the horizon. Most trekkers reach the summit before noon. Return to Kyanjin Gompa for the night.

D6

Optional: Tserko Ri Summit Day

OPTIONAL SUMMIT · 5–6 HRS ROUND TRIP

▲ 4,984m · +1,114m gain

For the fit and acclimatized, Tserko Ri offers even more expansive views including Shishapangma in Tibet and — on perfectly clear days — Everest's distinctive pyramid. Depart pre-dawn for the summit at sunrise. The ascent is steep and demands good fitness. This is one of the highest viewpoints accessible without mountaineering equipment in the Langtang area.

D7

Kyanjin Gompa to Lama Hotel

TREK · 20 KM · 6–7 HRS DESCENT

▼ 2,380m · −1,490m loss

Begin the return. Descending the same valley feels surprisingly different — the views are reversed, and details missed on the way up suddenly reveal themselves. The long descent rewards with wildflower meadows and forest birdsong. Your knees will feel the grade — trekking poles are strongly recommended.

D8

Lama Hotel to Syabrubesi

TREK · 11 KM · 4–5 HRS DESCENT

▼ 1,460m · −920m loss

The final trekking day winds back through the gorge forest to Syabrubesi. Arrive by midday with time to celebrate your trek with a well-earned meal and cold drink. Overnight at a teahouse to prepare for the drive back to Kathmandu the next day.

D9

Syabrubesi to Kathmandu

DRIVE · 117 KM · 7–8 HOURS

▼ 1,400m

Return to Kathmandu by jeep or local bus. The journey gives you one last chance to soak in the mountain scenery. Arrive in the capital by late afternoon. Your Langtang Valley adventure is complete — memories of glaciers, yaks, and ancient monasteries will stay with you long after the mountain dust has washed away.

When to Go

Best Seasons for Langtang Trekking

Langtang is a year-round destination but conditions vary enormously. Plan around the seasons for the experience you want. For deeper planning, visit our best time to trek in Nepal resource.

🌸

Spring

March · April · May

Rhododendrons bloom in a spectacular cascade of red, pink, and white. Temperatures are mild, skies relatively clear before the monsoon builds. Trails are busy but not overcrowded. Wildlife is active, and the valley floor carpets itself in wildflowers. The best month overall is April.

★★★★★
Excellent — Best season
🌧️

Monsoon

June · July · August

Heavy rainfall makes trails slippery, leeches appear in lower forests, and mountain views are frequently obscured by clouds. Landslide risk increases on the approach road. Not recommended for most trekkers; however, the valley stays lush and green, and hardy trekkers find solitude with very few other visitors.

★★★★★
Poor — Not recommended
🍂

Autumn

September · October · November

The prime trekking window. Post-monsoon skies are the clearest of the year; the air scrubbed crystal by the rains. Mountain views are exceptional. Temperatures are comfortable, trails firm, and teahouses well-stocked. October is the single best month for the Langtang Valley trek. Trails are busiest during this period.

★★★★★
Excellent — Peak season
❄️

Winter

December · January · February

Cold but often stable and beautifully clear. Snow can block higher sections above 4,000m, particularly January–February. The lower trail to Lama Hotel remains accessible year-round. Kyanjin Gompa sees sub-zero temperatures overnight. Ganza La and Laurebina Pass are closed in deep winter. Offers unique winter landscape photography.

★★★★★
Fair — Experienced trekkers only

Entry Requirements

Permits & Entry Fees

All trekkers entering Langtang National Park require two permits. These can be arranged in Kathmandu at the Nepal Tourism Board office or, in some cases, at checkpoints en route.

🏔️

National Park Entry Permit

NPR 3,000 (~USD 22)

Required for all foreign nationals entering Langtang National Park. Available at the Nepal Tourism Board office in Kathmandu (Bhrikutimandap) or at the park entrance at Dhunche. SAARC nationals pay NPR 1,500. Valid for the duration of your trek.

🪪

TIMS Card

NPR 2,000 (~USD 15)

Trekkers' Information Management System card provides accident and security tracking. Individual trekkers (without a registered agency) pay NPR 2,000; trekkers with a registered agency pay NPR 1,000. Mandatory for all foreign trekkers in Nepal.

📋

Additional Permits

Varies by route

The Langtang–Helambu circuit may require an additional Shivapuri Nagarjun National Park permit (~NPR 250) if entering via Sundarijal. No special restricted area permits are needed for standard Langtang routes. Gosaikunda requires the standard NP entry only.

💡 Pro Tip on Permits

Book your permits through a registered trekking agency and you'll pay half price on the TIMS card (NPR 1,000 vs NPR 2,000). Permits are checked at multiple checkpoints — always carry originals, not photocopies. Our Langtang valley trek packages include all permit arrangements as standard.

Expert Advice

10 Essential Trekking Tips

Hard-won knowledge from experienced guides and seasoned trekkers to make your Langtang adventure safer and more rewarding.

01

Acclimatize Properly

Never ascend more than 500m per day above 3,000m. Build a rest day at Kyanjin Gompa before attempting Kyanjin Ri or Tserko Ri. Recognize AMS symptoms early: headache, nausea, fatigue, dizziness. The golden rule — if in doubt, go down. Read our detailed guide on Is Langtang Valley trek safe for comprehensive altitude safety advice.

02

Hire a Local Guide

A licensed Tamang or Sherpa guide does more than navigate — they share the valley's history and ecology, communicate with teahouse owners, and can manage emergencies. Post-2015 earthquake, supporting the local economy matters enormously. Solo trekking is permitted but a guide greatly enhances the experience and safety.

03

Start Early Each Day

Mountain afternoons often bring cloud buildup. Leave teahouses by 7–8am to hike in clear morning light, arriving at your destination before 1pm. This also leaves afternoon time for acclimatization walks and photography as the angle of light improves again near sunset.

04

Budget for Extra Costs

Beyond package costs, budget USD 15–20/day for personal spending: hot showers (NPR 200–500), charging devices (NPR 200–500 per charge at altitude), tea and snacks, souvenirs, and tips for guides and porters. WiFi is available but expensive and unreliable above Langtang Village.

05

Drink Constantly

Dehydration accelerates altitude sickness and fatigue. Aim for 3–4 litres of water per day. Always purify water from streams or taps using a filter or iodine tablets — giardia is a real risk. Hydration tablets or electrolyte powder help replace minerals lost through exertion and altitude.

06

Respect Local Culture

Always walk clockwise around mani walls, stupas, and prayer wheels. Remove shoes before entering monasteries. Ask permission before photographing people. Learn a few words of Tamang or Nepali — "Namaste" (hello), "Dhanyabad" (thank you) — these gestures mean the world to local communities.

07

Solo Trekkers: Plan Ahead

Langtang is one of Nepal's friendlier regions for solo trekking. The main trail is well-marked and busy in season. However, the Ganja La and off-season routes require a guide or strong navigation experience. For comprehensive planning, see our best treks for solo trekkers in Nepal guide with safety tips and route recommendations.

08

Get Travel Insurance

Non-negotiable. Ensure your policy covers trekking above 4,000m and includes helicopter evacuation. Medical evacuation from Kyanjin Gompa can cost USD 3,000–5,000 without insurance. Providers like World Nomads, True Traveller, and IMG offer Nepal-specific trekking cover. Carry your policy documents and insurer's emergency number.

09

Minimize Plastic Waste

Langtang National Park bans single-use plastics. Carry a reusable water bottle and filter — this saves money and waste. The valley's trash is carried out by porters or burned, both of which harm a fragile ecosystem. Pack out what you pack in, support teahouses that have solar water heating and waste management systems.

10

Compare with Other Treks

If you're weighing Langtang against other options, the Manaslu Circuit trek offers a more remote and challenging high-altitude circuit, while the Annapurna region is more developed. For a fuller picture, the Langtang Valley trek guide is an excellent reference for detailed planning.

Safety Information

Safety Guide & Warnings

Langtang is generally safe and well-managed, but altitude, weather, and terrain demand respect. Know the risks before you go.

Altitude Mountain Sickness (AMS)

AMS is the most common health risk above 3,500m. Symptoms include persistent headache (not relieved by ibuprofen), nausea, dizziness, loss of appetite, and insomnia. High-Altitude Pulmonary Edema (HAPE) and High-Altitude Cerebral Edema (HACE) are life-threatening emergencies requiring immediate descent. Always acclimatize properly and recognize early symptoms.

For comprehensive information about trekking risks and the post-earthquake trail status, our dedicated page covering Is Langtang Valley trek safe answers the most important questions with up-to-date information.

Trail & Weather Hazards

Landslides are possible on the road to Syabrubesi during and after monsoon. Snow can block higher passes November–March. Flash floods occur in the gorge section. Always check weather forecasts (Windy.com and weather apps work at lower altitudes), and ask teahouse owners for local conditions before proceeding above Kyanjin Gompa.

The 2015 Earthquake Legacy

The April 2015 earthquake triggered an avalanche that destroyed most of Langtang Village, killing over 250 people. The rebuilt trail and teahouses are structurally sound, and the earthquake memorial at Langtang Village is a moving reminder of both the tragedy and the community's resilience. Rockfall awareness remains important in the gorge sections.

🚨
Descend Immediately If:

Symptoms of HAPE or HACE appear — severe breathlessness at rest, confusion, inability to walk in a straight line, or loss of consciousness. Do not wait until morning. Descend immediately regardless of time of day or night.

⚠️
Ganja La Pass Warning:

Never attempt the Ganja La Pass (5,122m) without a guide, crampons, and rope. Several trekkers have died attempting this crossing without proper equipment or preparation. The pass is closed November–April.

⚠️
Road Conditions:

The road from Dhunche to Syabrubesi is unpaved and prone to landslides June–September. Confirm road conditions with your driver and allow extra travel time. Jeep is strongly preferred over local bus.

ℹ️
Emergency Contacts:

Tourist Police Helpline: 1144 · Nepal Red Cross: +977-1-4270650 · CIWEC Clinic Kathmandu: +977-1-4435232 · Emergency Helicopter Rescue: Contact your insurance company's 24hr line immediately.

ℹ️
Mobile Coverage:

Ncell and Nepal Telecom provide reasonable coverage to Lama Hotel. Signal is spotty between Lama Hotel and Langtang Village. Kyanjin Gompa has intermittent coverage. Satellite communicators (Garmin InReach) are recommended for the Ganja La route.

Frequently Asked Questions

Langtang Trek: Common Questions

Everything first-time and returning trekkers ask about the Langtang Valley routes.

How difficult is the Langtang Valley trek for a beginner?

The classic Langtang Valley route is rated moderate and is very achievable for a healthy first-timer with a few months of preparation. The main challenge is altitude — reaching 3,870m at Kyanjin Gompa and optionally 4,773m at Kyanjin Ri. Build fitness with regular hiking and stair climbing in the months before, and follow a sensible acclimatization schedule. A guide helps enormously. See the comprehensive Langtang Valley trek guide for a beginner-focused breakdown of what to expect.

What is the difference between Kyanjin Ri and Tserko Ri?

Both are viewpoint summits accessible as day hikes from Kyanjin Gompa. Kyanjin Ri (4,773m) is closer, more commonly climbed, and takes 4–5 hours round trip — suitable for most acclimatized trekkers. Tserko Ri (4,984m) is higher, takes 5–7 hours round trip, is steeper, and offers wider views including into Tibet and theoretically Everest on very clear days. Most trekkers do Kyanjin Ri on Day 1 at Kyanjin and attempt Tserko Ri on Day 2 if feeling strong.

Can I do the Langtang trek without a guide?

Yes — the main trail to Kyanjin Gompa is well-marked, teahouse infrastructure is excellent, and independent trekking is fully permitted. You'll still need a TIMS card and National Park permit. However, for the Ganja La crossing, the Gosaikunda route, and any trekking off the main trail, a guide is strongly recommended. Solo trekkers should also be aware of the heightened risks; consult our best treks for solo trekkers in Nepal guide for solo-specific advice and safety tips.

How do I get from Kathmandu to Syabrubesi?

There are three main options: (1) Private jeep arranged through your trekking company — the most comfortable option, takes 7–8 hours, costs USD 80–120 for the vehicle. (2) Local bus from Machhapokhari Bus Park (Gongabu) in Kathmandu — departures around 6–7am, takes 8–9 hours, costs NPR 600–800. (3) Tourist bus to Dhunche then local transport — a middle-ground option. The road passes through Trishuli Bazaar and Dhunche. Jeep is recommended as it handles the rough final section better and can be arranged to stop at viewpoints.

Is teahouse accommodation comfortable?

Teahouses have improved dramatically since 2015 rebuilding. Most offer private double rooms, though shared facilities (toilets, washing) remain common at higher altitudes. Rooms are basic but clean, with warm blankets provided. At Kyanjin Gompa, expect temperatures to drop significantly overnight — sleeping bags are recommended even in autumn. Hot showers are available for a surcharge (NPR 200–500). Wi-Fi exists in most teahouses below 3,500m but is slow and often unavailable above Langtang Village.

How does Langtang compare to Everest Base Camp or Annapurna?

Langtang offers a shorter, less crowded, and more affordable alternative to the famous EBC and Annapurna Circuit treks. It reaches similar or higher altitudes (Tserko Ri at 4,984m vs Thorong La at 5,416m) but in a more intimate, community-feel setting. The wildlife and cultural diversity is arguably richer per km walked. If you're comparing routes and considering more remote options, the Manaslu Circuit trek is another excellent less-crowded alternative at higher altitudes. For planning across all regions, the travel guide provides a useful comparative overview.

What food is available on the trail?

Dal bhat (rice, lentils, vegetables, pickle, papadum) is the staple and is nutritionally excellent for trekking — unlimited refills are standard practice. Teahouses also serve pasta, noodles, fried rice, momos (dumplings), omelettes, pancakes, porridge, and soups. Vegetarian options are plentiful. Meat becomes less reliable at altitude; vegetarian is the safer choice above Langtang Village. Fresh yak cheese from the Kyanjin factory is a genuine local highlight. Energy bars and snacks from your pack supplement between teahouse meals.
